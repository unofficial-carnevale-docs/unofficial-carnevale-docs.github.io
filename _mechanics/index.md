## Tags

A lot of the analysis presented here are dependant on Tags. To be able to compare models, we tried to give them Tags to group them by role. These tags were validated by ensuring their computed capability doesn't sit far from their role's standard.

### Tag list

- **Tank** — high resistance, not necessarily high damage; role is to absorb enemy attacks and create space for allies; protects by being a compulsory threat that controls enemies or forces them to engage it.
- **Brawler** — melee model built to stay in a fight; resistance is PRIMARY (longevity defines the role); damage is a meaningful SECONDARY; distinguished from Damage Dealer by the emphasis on sustained presence over damage output.
- **Damage Dealer** — primary role is damage output; resistance is secondary and functional (just enough to reach and maintain combat); focused on sustained damage over time, not burst. Add adjectives: melee / mid-range / long range / single target / AoE / anti-armor / etc.
- **Assassin** — burst damage + target selection ability; selects targets via high mobility (Move 5+, Flight, Infiltration) for melee, or via ranged attack; does not prioritize survival; distinguished from Damage Dealer by burst vs. sustained output and independent target selection.
- **Objective Runner** — mobility and scenario-focused.
- **Control** — primary design intent is hindering the opponent: stun, forced movement, placement disruption, etc. A single control element (one Stun weapon, one Grapple ability) is not enough — use Line Fighter (control) instead.
- **Line Fighter** — basic combat model valued for cost and board presence rather than a distinctive tactical function; can take adjectives for nuance (e.g. anti-armor, control) without elevating to a full specialized role.
- **Support** — PRIMARY function is enabling/healing/buffing allies; incidentally having an aura does not qualify; the model must be designed around supporting others.
- **Specialist** — for edge cases; always paired with descriptive adjectives.

### Mage exception

Mages may carry dual role tags when their disciplines span multiple roles (e.g. Blood Rites → Damage Dealer, Divinity → Support). A single-discipline mage gets one tag matching the discipline's function.

### Drown vs. forcing-into-water

Forcing enemies into water is Control; performing the Drown action itself (damage) makes the model a Damage Dealer or Specialist, not Control.
