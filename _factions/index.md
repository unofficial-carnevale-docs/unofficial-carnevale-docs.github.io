<h2 id="the-vatican" class="faction-head"><img class="faction-icon" src="/assets/factions/vatican.png" alt=""> The Vatican</h2>

*An elite melee-and-magic faction: above-average dealers and brawlers on the game's best anti-armour-piercing armored characters, backed by the deepest support and magic bench in the game.*

The Vatican is almost entirely a melee faction, with a deep line of brawlers and the best durability against armour-piercing enemies in the game: a large share of the gang carries Universal Shielding. That anti-piercing armour does nothing against flat damage, drowning, or life-loss spells however, and the faction is weak in water: it has only one dedicated Canal Fighter, and the Full Plate rule makes its hard-hitting knights vulnerable near the canals.

Off the combat line, it has the deepest magic and support bench in the game: nine mages across all five disciplines, a dozen support models, and the most layered Will-Point economy of any faction. It is light on Command Points, slow on foot, and a significant part of the gang cannot score objectives. A Construct sub-engine provides fast flying models as a counterweight. The roster is mostly mid-cost elites with very few cheap fillers, and it is not the strongest faction for scenario play.

<h2 id="the-guild" class="faction-head"><img class="faction-icon" src="/assets/factions/guild.png" alt=""> The Guild</h2>

*A flexible, Will-Point-rich toolbox with the best damage dealers in the game and a strong scenario/theft game — but fragile bodies and no real tanks.*

The Guild has the most Will Points in the game, and every model benefits from Companion, whether innate or granted by their Leader. It fields the best damage dealers in the game, including the single hardest-hitting model, across melee and magic in all five disciplines.

It is also the strongest faction at scenario play and disruption, with objective runners, fast harassers, and pickpockets, all backed by strong mobility. Its main weakness is fragility: no tanks exist anywhere in the roster, and bodies go down quickly under focused pressure. A broad cost range lets it build as a tight elite gang or a larger swarm.

<h2 id="strigoi" class="faction-head"><img class="faction-icon" src="/assets/factions/strigoi.png" alt=""> Strigoi</h2>

*A self-sustaining Frenzied melee attrition faction: cold numbers look ordinary, but spending life as a resource for bursts plus healing-on-kill make its fighters relentless. Immune to Will-Point denial, but a few dedicated counterpicks exists in other factions (notably Vatican).*

Strigoi runs on the Frenzy engine: nearly every fighter can burst by spending Life Points as Will Points and recover them by hitting, on deep life pools. Unboosted it is only average, and every burst costs the life it then has to recover, so life management can be demanding against hard-hitting gangs. The faction is vulnerable to burst damage, but completely immune to Will-Point denial.

In terms of roster composition, it is elite melee with strong mobility through flight and infiltration, built around alpha strikes, with a few cheap thralls to fill out the gang. It is weak at scenario play.

<h2 id="rashaar" class="faction-head"><img class="faction-icon" src="/assets/factions/rashaar.png" alt=""> Rashaar</h2>

*The true masters of the cannals, built around its canal fighters and monstrous centerpieces — it drowns, grapples and controls the enemy off the board — but a third of the roster can't score.*

Rashaar's strength lives in the water. It has the game's biggest squad of canal fighters backed by huge durable monsters, and although its few dry-land damage dealers are only average, the water fighters hit harder to compensate for being confined to the canals. The faction has the widest cost range in the game, from cheap fodder up to giant anchor models.

It is the premier control faction: Stun, grapples, repositioning, and locking down the enemy are very common in the roster. They also have a lot of Fear that pressures low-Mind gangs (though it is ineffective against Brave models). Its Soul Drain is the most efficient Will Point command in the game.

The weaknesses are structural: A third of the gang is Mindless and cannot score Primary Objectives. The kit leans heavily on water, and drier boards or being forced onto land blunt its mobility and drowning game. Points-dense monster centrepieces also invite focus-fire.

<h2 id="patricians" class="faction-head"><img class="faction-icon" src="/assets/factions/patricians.png" alt=""> Patricians</h2>

*An alpha-strike gunline with the game's best command economy and the cleanest scoring. Its cold sustained damage looks low because its damage is front-loaded into snipers and one-turn bursts.*

Patricians has the most Command Points in the game with a faction command that grants +1 Action Point. Its offence is built around alpha strikes: snipers, including the single hardest-hitting shoter in the game, and burst gunners fuelled by a rich Will-Point economy. It is also the cleanest scoring faction in the game, with the widest range of leaders, from the cheapest support leader in the game to a premium command hub.

The downside is low sustained damage. The roster is heavily range-dependent and susceptible to rush tactics, and its shooting is reload-gated, often limited to one shot per turn. Most models are slow on foot. Certain model combinations are very powerful but expensive, though costs are otherwise well-balanced across the roster.

<h2 id="the-doctors" class="faction-head"><img class="faction-icon" src="/assets/factions/doctors.png" alt=""> The Doctors</h2>

*Durable, Nexus-powered damage and control, and the second best faction in the water. But a huge share of Mindless models makes them the worst scorers in the game.*

The Doctors combine damage and durability better than any other faction. The roster includes a deep dealer line, a good squad of canal fighters, several very tanky models, and some monsters, all backed by heavy control through Stun and grapples. Attrition is a strength, with tough bodies and sustained damage reinforcing each other, and it is a strong magic faction with seven mages covering all five disciplines.

Its engine is the Nexus: models share pooled Will Points, allowing heavy burst use, but with very few ways to regenerate. This forces the gang to clump together and makes it vulnerable to focus-fire on the Nexus holder. Beyond that, it is the worst primary scoring faction in the game with around 40% Mindless models, many bodies are unreliable due to Primitive, and the gang as a whole is slow. The cost range is wide, from cheap fodder to large expensive beasts.

<h2 id="gifted" class="faction-head"><img class="faction-icon" src="/assets/factions/gifted.png" alt=""> Gifted</h2>

*A mercenary masquerade troupe built around unique gimmicks. Elite-priced and Command-Point-starved, it's a faction of super specialists.*

Gifted members can be recruited by any faction, this desin makes their roster very unique: Gifted is a specialist toolbox, offering tools no other faction can replicate: Mask-givers, summoning, charge protection,... It has decent elite dealers and solid mobility and objective pieces. For initiative, it compensates for its lack of Command Points through its faction command.

The weaknesses are significant. It has the smallest native roster, with few tanks, weak control, and low anti-armour. Mask-givers are very weak models designed only to grant unique buffs to others, which reduces the number of genuinely viable models further. The faction is gimmick-dependent: it can be exceptional at specific tasks and hard to counter, but requires experience to have the right piece in the right place, and is vulnerable to target focus. It is elite-priced with the fewest cheap models of any faction, and roughly average at scenario play.
