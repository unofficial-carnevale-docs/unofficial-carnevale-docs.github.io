### Cantrip of the Chariot

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6" that isn't in base contact with an enemy. That character immediately makes a **Run/Climb** action that cannot move into base contact with an enemy.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Grants a friendly model a free Run/Climb without spending their AP, at 0 WP. The no-charge restriction matters — it is a repositioning tool, not a free charge delivery. AP impact: gives a model one free movement action; strong for objective contesting or escape. Scarcity: free movement via spell is partially covered by CP ORDER commands, but cheaper here (0 WP vs 1 CP).
</div>

### Renewed Vigour

Pick (X) friendly characters in line of sight within 12". Those characters remove any **Stunned** counters they have, and cannot gain **Stunned** counters until the end of the round.

<div class="callout">
<p class="callout-title">FAQ</p>

**Can I use Renewed Vigour to remove a Stunned counter from a character with the Full Plate special rule?** Renewed Vigour can be used to *prevent* a Full Plate character from gaining a Stunned counter when they enter water. It cannot remove the Stunned counter already caused by Full Plate — that counter is only removed when the character ends one of its turns out of water.

</div>

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Un-Stuns 2 friendly models and grants Stun immunity for the rest of the round. Each un-Stunned model recovers its full activation — potentially 3 AP each = 6 AP recovered. This is the highest AP-swing spell in the game against Stun-heavy opponents. Diff 6 (94%) and 12" range make it very accessible. Scarcity: Stun immunity is unique to Runes. Replaceability: nothing else grants Stun immunity.
</div>

### Fiery Rhetoric

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 1". That character replenishes **1 Command Point**.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Recovers 1 CP on a friendly model within 1". CP enables initiative (PLAN), extra actions (ORDER), reactions (COUNTER), and auras (ABILITY). Recovering 1 CP mid-round can flip initiative or trigger a key command. Diff 7 (87%). The near-contact range exposes the caster significantly. Scarcity: CP recovery via spell is unique.
</div>

### Waves of Force

Pick a point in water in line of sight within 6". Then pick another point within line of sight of the caster and 6" of the first point. Trace an imaginary line between the two points. If that line does not pass through Impassable Terrain, any character touched by that line no higher than 3" above the first point gets hit by a **Grapple** action, with the roll equal to the number of aces in the Magic Roll.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Requires a navigable line across water, decent MIND to generate Aces (Aces = Grapple roll result), and careful positioning. With 1/3 of every board being water, the terrain requirement is met in most games — this spell is less situational than it first appears. Pushes models into deep water for drowning risk. Diff 8 (76%) and the precise positioning setup are the real constraints. FF: any character on the line is hit. Scarcity: ranged forced movement via spell is unique.
</div>

### Walk Between Worlds

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". That character gains **Ethereal**, **Flight**, and **Slippery** until the end of its next turn.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Ethereal + Flight + Slippery until end of target's next turn — the strongest mobility package deliverable via spell. Diff 6 (94%) at 2 WP is reliable. AP impact: massive — enables any model to reach any position regardless of terrain. Best on a fast model contesting a deep objective or escaping melee. Scarcity: the three-ability combination via spell is unique. Ethereal/Flight/Slippery confirmed in rules_abilities.md (ignore terrain · fly without a roll · never hit by a disengage AoO).
</div>

### Ice Lock

Place the **Blast** marker in water in line of sight within 8". Any characters at least partially over the **Blast** marker receive a **Stunned** counter and are moved the shortest distance until they're not over the **Blast** marker. The area under the **Blast** marker is treated as solid ground. Remove the **Blast** marker at the end of the round.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Places solid ground over water — Stuns and ejects all characters under the template, then freezes that area as solid ground until end of round. Breaks swimming routes, strands models in awkward positions. The solid ground can also serve as a landing zone for friendly models. FF: Stun hits friendlies under template too. With 1/3 of every board being water, the water requirement is met in every game — this is a reliable option, not a niche one. Scarcity: water-to-solid terrain transformation is unique; Stun + terrain control combination is not replicated elsewhere.
</div>

### Madness

Pick one enemy character in line of sight within 3". That character immediately makes an action. For the purpose of the action, the enemy character counts as a friendly character, with the caster's player deciding where to move them and making any rolls or additional actions (such as Attacks of Opportunity). This does not count towards their **3AP** for the round. Always ask your opponent to handle their own models!

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
The highest-ceiling spell in the game. Forces an enemy model to take one full action under the caster's control, without counting toward their AP. Potential outcomes: attack allies, walk off objectives, move into water. Diff 8 + 2 WP + 3" range — high cost, high risk, irreplaceable reward. AP impact: +1 effective AP to caster side, −1 to enemy = +2 net swing. Scarcity: full action control is completely irreplaceable; nothing else in the game does this. Replaceability: zero.
</div>
