### Cantrip of the Chariot

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6" that isn't in base contact with an enemy. That character immediately makes a **Run/Climb** action that cannot move into base contact with an enemy.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Gives a friendly model a free Run/Climb without costing them any AP or the caster any WP. The no-charge restriction is important: this is a repositioning tool, not a delivery mechanism for a free charge. Its value is in objective contesting, escape, or closing a gap without burning activation AP. CP ORDER commands can also grant extra movement, but at 1 CP vs 0 WP this is cheaper.
</div>

### Renewed Vigour

Pick (X) friendly characters in line of sight within 12". Those characters remove any **Stunned** counters they have, and cannot gain **Stunned** counters until the end of the round.

<div class="callout">
<p class="callout-title">FAQ</p>

**Can I use Renewed Vigour to remove a Stunned counter from a character with the Full Plate special rule?** Renewed Vigour can be used to *prevent* a Full Plate character from gaining a Stunned counter when they enter water. It cannot remove the Stunned counter already caused by Full Plate. That counter is only removed when the character ends one of its turns out of water.

</div>

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Removes Stun from up to 2 friendly models and makes them immune to Stun for the rest of the round. Each un-Stunned model recovers a full activation (potentially 3 AP each, for a net 6 AP swing), making this the highest AP-recovery spell in the game against Stun-heavy opponents. At Diff 6 (94%) and 12" range it is very accessible. Stun immunity via spell is unique to Runes of Sovereignty. Nothing else provides it.
</div>

### Fiery Rhetoric

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 1". That character replenishes **1 Command Point**.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Replenishes 1 CP on a friendly model within 1". A mid-round CP can flip initiative (PLAN), enable an extra action (ORDER), trigger a reaction (COUNTER), or sustain an aura (ABILITY), with broad downstream effects. Diff 7 (87%) is reasonable. The 1" range is the real cost: the caster needs to be nearly in contact with the recipient, which is a significant positional risk. No other spell recovers CP.
</div>

### Waves of Force

Pick a point in water in line of sight within 6". Then pick another point within line of sight of the caster and 6" of the first point. Trace an imaginary line between the two points. If that line does not pass through Impassable Terrain, any character touched by that line no higher than 3" above the first point gets hit by a **Grapple** action, with the roll equal to the number of aces in the Magic Roll.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Requires careful geometry: a navigable water line, MIND to generate Aces (which become the Grapple roll result), and the right positioning. With roughly a third of every board being water, the terrain requirement is met in most games, making this less situational than it first looks. The real constraints are Diff 8 (76%) and the setup precision needed to hit the right models. Friendly fire is a genuine risk: anything on the line gets hit. Ranged forced movement through a spell is unique to Runes of Sovereignty.
</div>

### Walk Between Worlds

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". That character gains **Ethereal**, **Flight**, and **Slippery** until the end of its next turn.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
The strongest mobility package deliverable by spell: Ethereal (ignore terrain), Flight (fly without a roll), and Slippery (never hit by a disengage AoO) until end of the target's next turn. At Diff 6 (94%) and 2 WP it is reliable. Put it on a fast model to reach a deep objective, escape melee, or cross any terrain configuration the board throws at it. The three-ability combination in a single cast doesn't exist anywhere else.
</div>

### Ice Lock

Place the **Blast** marker in water in line of sight within 8". Any characters at least partially over the **Blast** marker receive a **Stunned** counter and are moved the shortest distance until they're not over the **Blast** marker. The area under the **Blast** marker is treated as solid ground. Remove the **Blast** marker at the end of the round.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Converts a water area into solid ground until end of round, Stunning and ejecting everyone under the template in the process. It breaks swimming routes, strands models in awkward positions, and the new solid ground can serve as a landing zone for your own models. Friendly fire on the Stun is real. With roughly a third of every board being water, this is a reliable pick rather than a situational one. The water-to-solid terrain conversion combined with Stun and forced movement is not replicated by any other spell.
</div>

### Madness

Pick one enemy character in line of sight within 3". That character immediately makes an action. For the purpose of the action, the enemy character counts as a friendly character, with the caster's player deciding where to move them and making any rolls or additional actions (such as Attacks of Opportunity). This does not count towards their **3AP** for the round. Always ask your opponent to handle their own models!

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
The highest-ceiling spell in the game. Forces an enemy model to take one full action under the caster's control, without it counting toward their AP: attack an ally, walk off an objective, move into water. The AP swing is +2 net (one gained for your side, one taken from theirs). Diff 8, 2 WP, and a 3" trigger range make this expensive and risky to set up. Full action control over an enemy is completely irreplaceable. Nothing else in the game comes close.
</div>
