### Cantrip of the Sun

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". That character replenishes **1 Life Point** and **1 Will Point**.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
The only cantrip that recovers WP: spending 1 AP at net 0 WP cost to gain +1 LP and refill a Will Point is a unique economic trick. A Fumble here is especially punishing: you Stun the caster and lose an extra WP on a spell cast specifically to recover one. Diff 7 (87% at MIND 4) is the main risk. Nothing else in the game does this at 0 WP cost.
</div>

### Protection of the Eye

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". That character gains **Universal Shielding (X)+3** until the end of its next turn.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Universal Shielding (X+3 = 5 at Mage 2) is **not** flat Protection. It sets a minimum Protection floor that only activates when an attacker's Penetration would push effective Protection below that floor, and it is hard-capped at the target's starting Protection. Against a no-Penetration attacker on a model already at its starting Protection, this does nothing. The "5" ceiling is only meaningful on models with starting Protection ≥5. In practice this is anti-Penetration insurance: deploy it specifically against Blood Rites casters, post–Sunder Armour alpha strikes, or any Pen-heavy threat. Against anything else it contributes zero. Diff 5 (97%) makes it reliable to land, so the question is purely whether the matchup warrants it. No other spell grants Universal Shielding as an ability.
</div>

### Eldritch Armour

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". That character gains +(X) PROTECTION until the end of its next turn.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
+2 Protection translates to roughly +0.8 LP saved per incoming attack at the median (capped by incoming damage). The stacking cap of 3 prevents extremes. Simpler and more broadly useful than Protection of the Eye: it works against any attacker, not just Pen-heavy ones, but often slightly weaker in practice because flat Protection is more widely available and equipment can achieve the same result.
</div>

### Rejuvenation

Total up every **Ace** rolled plus (X). Replenish this many **Life Points** in total from any number of characters within 12" in line of sight, distributing the amount between the characters as you wish.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
The most flexible healing spell in the game: freely distribute the total across as many LOS friendlies within 12" as you like. Output scales with dice (Aces+2 at Mage 2), so MIND matters significantly. Diff 7 (87%) is moderate risk, but the 12" range keeps the caster well out of danger. Free-distribution multi-target healing doesn't exist anywhere else.
</div>

### Holy Light

All enemy characters with the **Mage** ability within 6" receive a **Stunned** counter and lose (X) **Life Points**.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Entirely binary in value: exceptional against magic gangs (2 LP loss + Stun on every enemy Mage in the 6" AoE, LP bypassing Protection entirely), worthless against gangs with no Mages. Stun denies full activations: Stunning 2 enemy Mages is potentially the highest AP swing in the game (~6 AP denied). Diff 8 (76%) is a real cost for a spell this situational. It is the only anti-Mage AoE in the game and completely irreplaceable when the matchup calls for it.
</div>

### Defender of Destiny

All friendly characters within 6" gain **Parry (X)** and **Expert Protection (X)** until the end of the round.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Every friendly model within 6" gains Parry(2) and Expert Protection(2) until end of round, a duration longer than most buffs. The AoE delivery is where the value multiplies: two or more models in range and this becomes one of the most impactful defensive casts available. Diff 8 is the barrier, so commit it before the opponent's key attacker activates. Glimpse of Glory (Fateweaving) offers the same two abilities but on a single target. This is the only multi-model version.
</div>

### Aqua Curitiva

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". Place the **Blast** marker under this character. At the end of the round, any friendly characters at least partially over the **Blast** marker replenish **(X)+2 Life Points**. Any enemy characters at least partially over the **Blast** marker receive a **Stunned** counter. Then remove the **Blast** marker.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Diff 8 and 2 WP makes this the heaviest investment in Divinity. The 4 LP heal is the highest single-event heal in the game, but end-of-round timing is a real penalty: the healed model can die before resolution, and enemies can simply walk out of the Stun zone before it triggers. Best reserved for static, defensive situations where neither side is moving much. The end-of-round delay should be treated as a significant discount on the stated value.
</div>
