### Cantrip of Justice

This character may immediately cast another magic spell for **0AP**. This spell costs **Will Points** as normal, but can be any spell known by any other mage (friendly or enemy) within line of sight.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Spends 1 AP to cast any known spell from any LOS mage at 0 AP — effectively a 2-spell turn for 1 AP. The copied spell still costs its full WP. Can target enemy mages' spells. Diff 7 (87% at MIND 4) — failure loses 1 AP and blocks further casts this turn. AP impact: the highest direct AP multiplier of any cantrip. Scarcity: spell copying is unique to Wild Magic. Replaceability: completely irreplaceable.
</div>

### They Sleep Underwater

Pick (X) friendly characters in line of sight within 12". Those characters gain **Fast Swimmer (X)** until the end of the round.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Fast Swimmer(2) to 2 friendly targets, Diff 5 (97%), 12" range. With 1/3 of every board being water, this is relevant in most games rather than a niche pick. Low WP investment for a potentially decisive speed advantage when crossing or contesting water. Scarcity: Fast Swimmer buff is unique to Wild Magic.
</div>

### Healing

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". That character replenishes **(X) Life Points**.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Simplest healing spell — fixed 2 LP, single friendly, Diff 5 (97%), 6" range. No conditions or splits; reliable. Best when 2 LP recovery needs to be guaranteed and risk of Diff 7 (Rejuvenation) is unacceptable. Scarcity: healing available across multiple disciplines; this is the most accessible version. Replaceability: partial.
</div>

### Summon Vermin

Pick one enemy character in line of sight within 12" and place the **Blast** marker over that character. Total up every ace plus (X), that character takes that much Damage and all other characters (friendly and enemy) under the marker take **(X)-1 Damage**.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Best 1-WP offensive spell on expected value (~4.4 Dmg primary at Mage 2/MIND 4). 12" range is the longest of any direct damage spell. Diff 5 (97%) makes it highly reliable. Dice-scaling output has high variance — low rolls disappoint. Splash (X-1 = 1 Dmg at Mage 2) under template is minor. No Penetration: full Protection applies (see effective damage table). FF: splash hits friendlies under template. Scarcity: highest-ceiling 1-WP damage spell is exclusive to Wild Magic.
</div>

### Gateway

Pick one character within 1". Remove this character and place them anywhere out of base contact on solid ground within 8". This removal or placement cannot cause Attacks of Opportunity.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Teleports a model within 1" to any solid ground within 8", no AoO on either removal or placement — the only AoO-free displacement in the game. Best use: extract a key model from melee before it dies, preserving its remaining AP. Can also reposition enemy models off objectives. Diff 8 (76%) + 1" trigger range are real barriers. Scarcity: AoO-free teleportation is irreplaceable. AP impact: can recover a model's remaining AP by extracting it from melee. Replaceability: Walk Between Worlds provides mobility but not teleportation.
</div>

### Groundsnap

Place the **Blast** marker on solid ground in line of sight within 8". Any characters at least partially over the **Blast** marker take (X) **Damage** and are moved the shortest distance until they're not under the **Blast** marker. The area under the **Blast** marker is treated as impassable terrain. Remove the **Blast** marker at the end of the round.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
2 Dmg + forced ejection + impassable zone on solid ground until end of round. Poor offensive output for 2 WP (no Pen, fixed 2, FF risk). Zone denial is the primary value — blocking a chokepoint, objective approach, or cover piece for a full round can shift scenario play. Diff 7 (87%) + 2 WP is reasonable for area control. Scarcity: impassable zone on solid ground is unique in Wild Magic. Verdict: weakest offensive 2-WP spell, scenario-context dependent.
</div>

### Sunder Armour

Pick one enemy character in line of sight within 6". That character receives a total of **-3 and -(X)** to its PROTECTION until the end of the round.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
−5 Protection at Mage 2 until end of round. Against a model with Protection 4: reduces to 0 Protection → next attacker faces zero dice. This is a force-multiplier, not a standalone spell — value is entirely dependent on a coordinated follow-up attack. Diff 7 (87%) + 2 WP. Scarcity: Protection reduction of this magnitude is unique to Wild Magic. AP impact: indirect — the follow-up attacker gains ~2.0 expected additional effective Damage (per Penetration Math section). At the real median Prot 3, −5 Protection strips armour to 0; most valuable against the Prot 4–5 tail and as a Penetration-stack enabler. Replaceability: no weapon or ability reduces Protection by 5 in one action.
</div>
