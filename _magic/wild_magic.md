### Cantrip of Justice

This character may immediately cast another magic spell for **0AP**. This spell costs **Will Points** as normal, but can be any spell known by any other mage (friendly or enemy) within line of sight.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Spends 1 AP to immediately cast any spell from any LOS mage at 0 AP (friendly or enemy), effectively creating a 2-spell turn for a single AP. The copied spell still costs its full WP. At Diff 7 (87% at MIND 4) a failure loses 1 AP and blocks further casts, which is a painful price for a cantrip. Still, the ability to copy any spell from any mage in line of sight is completely unique to Wild Magic and irreplaceable.
</div>

### They Sleep Underwater

Pick (X) friendly characters in line of sight within 12". Those characters gain **Fast Swimmer (X)** until the end of the round.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Grants Fast Swimmer(2) to 2 friendly models at Diff 5 (97%) and 12" range. With roughly a third of every board being water, this is relevant in most games rather than a situational pick. At 1 WP it is a low investment for a potentially decisive speed advantage when crossing or contesting water zones. No other spell grants Fast Swimmer.
</div>

### Healing

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". That character replenishes **(X) Life Points**.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
The simplest healing spell in the game: fixed 2 LP, one friendly model, Diff 5 (97%), 6" range. No conditions, no splits, no variance. Best when you need a guaranteed 2 LP recovery and the Diff 7 risk of Rejuvenation is unacceptable. Healing is available across multiple disciplines, making this the most accessible version rather than an exclusive one.
</div>

### Summon Vermin

Pick one enemy character in line of sight within 12" and place the **Blast** marker over that character. Total up every ace plus (X), that character takes that much Damage and all other characters (friendly and enemy) under the marker take **(X)-1 Damage**.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
The best 1-WP offensive spell by expected value (~4.4 Dmg primary at Mage 2/MIND 4), with the longest range of any direct damage spell at 12". Diff 5 (97%) makes it highly reliable. The flip side of dice-scaling output is variance: low rolls significantly disappoint, and there is no Penetration, so full Protection applies. The splash (X-1 Dmg at Mage 2 = 1) under the template is minor and risks friendlies. The highest-ceiling 1-WP damage spell in the game is exclusive to Wild Magic.
</div>

### Gateway

Pick one character within 1". Remove this character and place them anywhere out of base contact on solid ground within 8". This removal or placement cannot cause Attacks of Opportunity.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Picks up any model within 1" and places them on solid ground anywhere within 8", with no Attacks of Opportunity on either removal or placement, making it the only AoO-free displacement in the game. The primary use is extracting a key model from melee before it dies, preserving whatever AP it has left. It can also reposition enemy models off objectives. Diff 8 (76%) and the 1" trigger range are real barriers. Walk Between Worlds provides mobility, but it doesn't teleport. This is irreplaceable.
</div>

### Groundsnap

Place the **Blast** marker on solid ground in line of sight within 8". Any characters at least partially over the **Blast** marker take (X) **Damage** and are moved the shortest distance until they're not under the **Blast** marker. The area under the **Blast** marker is treated as impassable terrain. Remove the **Blast** marker at the end of the round.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Deals 2 Dmg, ejects all models off the template, and leaves an impassable zone on solid ground until end of round. As an offensive spell the output is poor (no Penetration, fixed 2 Dmg, and friendly-fire risk), but offense isn't the point. Blocking a chokepoint, objective approach, or key cover piece for a full round can significantly shift scenario play. At Diff 7 (87%) and 2 WP the cost is reasonable for area control. The impassable terrain zone on solid ground is unique to Wild Magic, but it only shines in the right scenario context.
</div>

### Sunder Armour

Pick one enemy character in line of sight within 6". That character receives a total of **-3 and -(X)** to its PROTECTION until the end of the round.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
−5 Protection (−3 flat plus −X at Mage 2) until end of round: at the median Protection of 3 that strips armour entirely, and against Prot 4–5 models it removes most of their defensive dice. This is a force-multiplier, not a standalone spell: the value is entirely in the follow-up attack that faces a near-defenceless target. Against a Prot 4 model, the next attacker gains roughly +2.0 expected effective Damage. Diff 7 (87%) and 2 WP. No weapon or ability in the game reduces Protection by 5 in a single action, making this irreplaceable as a setup tool.
</div>
