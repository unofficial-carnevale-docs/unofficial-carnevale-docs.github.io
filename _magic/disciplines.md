Each discipline defines a Mage's role in the gang, since all spells must come from the same school.

## Blood Rites

**Theme:** Direct harm, LP drain, and armour penetration. Every spell either deals damage, strips LP, or enables a model to deal more damage.

**What it does well:** It is the only discipline with meaningful Penetration on multiple spells (Pen -2 to -4). Against armoured targets, no other discipline matches its effective damage output.

**What it cannot do:** It has no utility except Abyssal Mist, which has high difficulty and is expensive.

**Role in the gang:** An aggressive direct damage dealer.

---

## Divinity

**Theme:** Sustain, protection, and punishment of enemy Mages.

**What it does well:** It offers the best and most varied healing toolkit, alongside defensive single-target and AoE buffs. The Cantrip of the Sun partially offsets WP expenditure, making the discipline's WP economy better than raw costs suggest. Holy Light is also the only anti-Mage AoE in the game.

**What it cannot do:** It has zero offensive output against non-magic opponents. Three of its seven spells sit at Difficulty 8 (Holy Light, Defender of Destiny, Aqua Curitiva) — the highest concentration of hard-to-land spells of any discipline.

**Role in the gang:** The best protector and healer, a full support mage with a situational anti-mage role.

---

## Fateweaving

**Theme:** Stat manipulation and dice re-rolls. Fateweaving does not deal damage or heal — it makes everything else more likely to succeed.

**What it does well:** It is the only discipline that directly manipulates dice results, with flexible stat buffs and debuffs. Glimpse of Glory grants the highest single-model combat buff package in the game, while Marksman's Fortune and Otherworldly Oddity provide niche utility unavailable anywhere else (Reload manipulation, terrain control).

**What it cannot do:** It has zero direct damage or healing. Its value is entirely dependent on having high-quality models to buff or debuff.

**Role in the gang:** A force multiplier that makes the gang's best models perform above their baseline, or a debuffer that hinders enemy models.

---

## Runes of Sovereignty — Control and action economy

**Theme:** Dominance over movement and actions, support or control by moving people around.

**What it does well:** Madness is the highest-ceiling single spell in the game and fully irreplaceable. The Cantrip of the Chariot and Fiery Rhetoric are the only spell-based sources of free movement and CP recovery, while Wave of Force is a flexible long-range effect that moves opponents and allies alike.

**What it cannot do:** It deals zero direct damage or healing, and most spells have very short range or require water to function.

**Role in the gang:** A disruptive controller or a movement-based support. Must be able to survive or be mobile enough to be where it needs to be.

---

## Wild Magic — Versatile generalist

**Theme:** Adaptability. Wild Magic covers damage, healing, debuffing, movement, teleportation, and spell-copying in a single school.

**What it does well:** The Cantrip of Justice is a unique way to expand the spells known by the caster. Summon Vermin is the best 1-WP damage spell on expected value, with the longest range of any damage spell. Sunder Armour is the best force-multiplier setup spell, enabling devastating follow-up attacks by Summon Vermin or any friendly model. Gateway is a very flexible and powerful movement spell.

**What it cannot do:** Its buff spell is very narrow, and it is not the best at any single thing.

**Role in the gang:** A flexible hybrid that adapts to what the gang lacks, very dependent on its base profile.
