### Cantrip of the Stars

Until the start of this character's next turn, you may re-roll the **Destiny Dice** once, even though not usually able to, for whatever roll you wish.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Re-rolls the Destiny Die once (not just for magic — for any roll) before the start of the caster's next turn. The Destiny Die controls Critical/Fumble for every roll, so a single re-roll can avert a Fumble on a crucial combat or spell cast. Diff 5 (97% at MIND 4). Scarcity: Destiny Die manipulation is unique to Fateweaving. AP impact: indirect but potentially enormous — averting a Fumble on a key roll is worth more than any direct effect.
</div>

### Marksman's Fortune

Pick one character (friendly or enemy) in line of sight within 18". Any weapons that character has with **Reload (X)** must add or subtract 1 from the (X) value until the end of the round (caster's choice).

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Irreplaceable in Reload matchups; worthless otherwise. Forcing an enemy's Reload counter up prevents reloading; reducing yours enables an extra shot. Diff 4, 18" range — essentially free when applicable. Plan around whether the enemy gang uses Reload(X) weapons. Scarcity: the only Reload-manipulation effect in the game.
</div>

### Otherworldly Oddity

Pick one terrain feature with a footprint of 6" or less in line of sight within 12" with no characters on or in it. Until the start of this character's next turn, any friendly characters making movement actions on or in that terrain feature gain **Acrobatic (X)**. Any enemy characters count that terrain feature as impassable terrain.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Dual effect on a terrain piece: friendlies gain Acrobatic(2) traversing it, enemies treat it as impassable. Diff 5, 12" range = very accessible. The combined positioning lever — block an enemy route while smoothing your own — has strong scenario value. Scarcity: terrain dual-effect (buff own/block enemy) is unique to Fateweaving. Duration is short (until caster's next turn) but the blocking can force key movement reroutes.
</div>

### Blessing of the Sky

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". Until the end of its next turn, that character gains +(X) to be distributed between its ATTACK, DEXTERITY, and/or MIND in any combination. A character may only be affected by Blessing of the Sky once at a time.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
+2 distributed freely across Attack, Dexterity, and/or Mind. Flexibility is the key asset — allocate to whichever stat matters most in the current situation. Diff 7 (87%). One-active-at-a-time cap prevents stacking. Buff math is calibrated to the real medians (Attack 3 / Dexterity 4 / Mind 3); allocate to whichever stat matters most. Scarcity: flexible stat distribution is unique; other disciplines boost specific stats only.
</div>

### Curse of the Rent

Pick one enemy character in line of sight within 6". Until the end of its next turn, that character receives -(X) to be distributed by the caster between its ATTACK, DEXTERITY, and/or MIND in any combination. A character may only be affected by Curse of the Rent once at a time.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Mirror of Blessing applied to an enemy. Applying −2 Attack to a key attacker is often more efficient than buffing your own model's defence (the effect is the same but targets the source). Combining Blessing + Curse in one activation can swing combat math significantly. Same Diff 7, same range. Scarcity: flexible enemy debuff is unique.
</div>

### Glimpse of Glory

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". That character gains **Parry (X)**, **Expert Offence (X)**, **Expert Marksman (X)**, and **Expert Protection (X)** until the end of their next turn.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Four combat abilities simultaneously (Parry 2 + Expert Offence 2 + Expert Marksman 2 + Expert Protection 2) — highest single-model combat multiplier in the game. Diff 8 (76%) is the bottleneck. 1 WP wasted on a failure is painful for such an activation-defining spell. Best reserved for the turn a key model is expected to fight or be fought. Scarcity: the 4-ability stack is unique.
</div>

### Fate's Bounty

Total up every **Ace** rolled plus (X). Until the start of this character's next turn, you may re-roll this many dice (not the Destiny Dice), for whatever rolls you wish.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
The most reliable 2-WP spell in the game (Diff 4 = 99% at MIND 4). Generates Aces+2 re-rolls (~4.8 average with MIND 4) usable on any non-Destiny dice. Best cast first in an activation, then applied to subsequent rolls. WP economy: ~2.02 WP per successful cast — best efficiency of any 2-WP spell. Scarcity: bulk free re-rolls on any roll are unique to Fateweaving. AP impact: indirect multiplier across all subsequent actions this turn.
</div>
