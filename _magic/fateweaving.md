### Cantrip of the Stars

Until the start of this character's next turn, you may re-roll the **Destiny Dice** once, even though not usually able to, for whatever roll you wish.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Grants one Destiny Die re-roll usable on any subsequent roll before the caster's next turn: not just magic, any roll at all. Since the Destiny Die controls Critical and Fumble for every action, a single re-roll can avert a Fumble on the most consequential roll of the turn. Diff 5 (97% at MIND 4) makes this essentially free. The indirect value can be enormous. Averting a Fumble on a key attack or cast is worth more than most direct effects. Destiny Die manipulation exists nowhere else in the game.
</div>

### Marksman's Fortune

Pick one character (friendly or enemy) in line of sight within 18". Any weapons that character has with **Reload (X)** must add or subtract 1 from the (X) value until the end of the round (caster's choice).

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Entirely irreplaceable in Reload matchups, completely worthless outside of them. Forcing an enemy's Reload counter up locks them out of their weapon, and reducing your own enables an extra shot. At Diff 4 and 18" range the cast is essentially free when applicable. Whether to take it is a list-building decision: does the expected opposition use Reload(X) weapons? It is the only Reload-manipulation effect in the game.
</div>

### Otherworldly Oddity

Pick one terrain feature with a footprint of 6" or less in line of sight within 12" with no characters on or in it. Until the start of this character's next turn, any friendly characters making movement actions on or in that terrain feature gain **Acrobatic (X)**. Any enemy characters count that terrain feature as impassable terrain.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Dual effect on a single terrain piece: your models gain Acrobatic(2) moving through it, theirs treat it as impassable. At Diff 5 and 12" range the cast is very accessible, and the combined positioning lever (blocking an enemy approach while opening your own) has strong scenario value. Duration only lasts until the caster's next turn, but forcing a movement reroute in a critical moment is often enough. This terrain dual-effect is unique to Fateweaving.
</div>

### Blessing of the Sky

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". Until the end of its next turn, that character gains +(X) to be distributed between its ATTACK, DEXTERITY, and/or MIND in any combination. A character may only be affected by Blessing of the Sky once at a time.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
+2 freely distributed across Attack, Dexterity, and/or Mind: flexibility is the asset. Push it into Attack for melee, Dexterity to survive a disengage, or Mind for a mage about to cast. The one-active-at-a-time cap prevents stacking. No other discipline offers this kind of free stat allocation. Everything else targets a fixed stat.
</div>

### Curse of the Rent

Pick one enemy character in line of sight within 6". Until the end of its next turn, that character receives -(X) to be distributed by the caster between its ATTACK, DEXTERITY, and/or MIND in any combination. A character may only be affected by Curse of the Rent once at a time.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
The mirror image of Blessing of the Sky, applied to an enemy. Subtracting −2 Attack from a key attacker is often more efficient than buffing your own model's defence: the math is identical, but targeting the source removes the threat entirely for the remainder of their turn. Combining Blessing and Curse in a single activation can swing a combat significantly. No other discipline offers a freely distributed enemy debuff.
</div>

### Glimpse of Glory

Pick one friendly character in line of sight within 6". That character gains **Parry (X)**, **Expert Offence (X)**, **Expert Marksman (X)**, and **Expert Protection (X)** until the end of their next turn.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
Four combat abilities at once (Parry 2, Expert Offence 2, Expert Marksman 2, and Expert Protection 2), the highest single-model combat multiplier in the game. Diff 8 (76%) is the real bottleneck, and a failed cast wastes 1 WP on a spell meant to define a key activation. Commit it on the turn a priority model is about to fight or be fought. The four-ability stack in a single cast exists nowhere else.
</div>

### Fate's Bounty

Total up every **Ace** rolled plus (X). Until the start of this character's next turn, you may re-roll this many dice (not the Destiny Dice), for whatever rolls you wish.

<div class="callout" markdown="1">
The most reliable 2-WP spell in the game at Diff 4 (99% at MIND 4). It generates a pool of re-rolls: Aces+2, averaging ~4.8 with MIND 4, usable on any non-Destiny dice for the rest of the activation. Cast it first, then spend the re-rolls on whatever follows. WP efficiency is the best of any 2-WP spell at ~2.02 WP per successful cast. Bulk free re-rolls applicable to any roll type don't exist anywhere else.
</div>
